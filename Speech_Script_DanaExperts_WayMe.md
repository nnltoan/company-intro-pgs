## Slide 6 — Case Study Deep Dive
*~2 minutes | Show problem-solving ability*

> Let me go a bit deeper into what made this project challenging — because it wasn't just about putting a dot on a map.
>
> The **challenge** was building a cross-platform app that delivers accurate, continuous GPS updates without draining the battery. We also needed complex geofencing logic — not just circles on a map, but customizable zones with instant push notifications. And we had to design a monetization model that actually converts free users to paying customers.
>
> **Our solution**: We built an intelligent location update system that adapts its frequency based on the user's movement and battery level. Moving fast? More frequent updates. Standing still? We back off to save power. For geofencing, we implemented a server-side engine with configurable polygonal zones — much more flexible than basic radius-based fencing. And for monetization, we designed a tiered model — monthly at $6.99, yearly at $49.99, with a 3-day free trial that gives users enough time to see the value.
>
> The **results**: 21,800 active users, 100% on-time delivery, and strong app store ratings. This project proved that our team can handle the full lifecycle of a location-based consumer product — from architecture to app store.

## Tips for Delivery

- **Pace**: Speak slightly slower than feels natural. Pauses after key numbers (896 projects... 100% on-time...) let them sink in.
- **Eye contact**: On slides 5-6 (GPS app), look at the audience, not the screen. These are your strongest slides — own them.
- **Slide 8-9 transition**: The scrolling photos create a natural visual pause. Use this moment to speak more personally and warmly.
- **Questions**: If time allows, invite questions after slide 6 (case study) — it shows confidence and keeps the conversation interactive.
- **Closing**: End with energy. Don't trail off. "Thank you for your time. I'm happy to take any questions." — then smile and wait.
